---
title: "Oaths of Information"
---

The document’s form is standard for the period: a Justice of the Peace administered the oath; it is recorded as a third-person narrative transcribed by a court official; and it has a mark in place of a signature because LeBlanc was illiterate.

It is also in French. So while Criminal Law in Lower Canada was British and often administered in English, sometimes cases were archived in French. This was especially true if the individual could not speak English.
